### **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Setup in PGP**

* **Important:** If your organization does not use Single Sign-On (SSO), you are **required** to set up Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to access PGP. You will be automatically prompted to complete MFA setup during your first login, and you cannot proceed to the platform until MFA is configured.
* If your organization uses SSO (Okta, Azure, PingID, Google, etc.), MFA is managed externally and you will not be prompted to set up MFA in PGP.
* This guide walks you through the enforced MFA setup process for non-SSO users.

### **Prerequisites**

* Access to your PGP account (username and password)
* A smartphone or device with an authenticator app (e.g., Google Authenticator, Authy, Microsoft Authenticator)

### **Automatic MFA Setup for Non-SSO Users**

* **First Login:** When you log in to PGP for the first time (and SSO is not enabled), you will be **required** to set up MFA. The setup process will begin automatically and you must complete it to access your account.
* **Follow the On-Screen Instructions:** PGP will display a QR code and a manual setup key. Open your authenticator app, scan the QR code (or enter the key), and enter the 6-digit code generated by your app to verify.
* **Save Recovery Codes:** After successful setup, you will receive recovery codes. Save these codes securely—they are required if you lose access to your authenticator device.
* **Access Granted:** Once MFA is configured, you will be able to use PGP. You will be prompted for a 6-digit code from your authenticator app each time you log in.

### **Using MFA to Sign In**

* After setting up MFA, you will be prompted for a 6-digit code from your authenticator app each time you log in.
* Enter the code to complete the login process.

### **Recovery Options**

* **Lost Device:** Use one of your saved recovery codes to log in and reset your MFA device.
* **No Recovery Codes:** Contact your PGP administrator or support for assistance.

### **Troubleshooting**

* **Code Not Working:** Ensure your device time is set to automatic/synced. Codes are time-based and will change every 30 seconds.
* **Cannot Scan QR Code:** Use the manual setup key provided during setup.
* **Lost All Access:** Contact PGP support for account recovery.

### **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

* **Is MFA optional?** No. If SSO is not enabled, MFA setup is **required** and enforced for all users.
* **Can I use SMS for MFA?** Currently, PGP supports authenticator apps for MFA. SMS-based MFA is not supported.
* **Can I disable MFA after enabling it?** No. MFA is required for all non-SSO users and cannot be disabled.
* **What if I lose my recovery codes?** Contact your PGP administrator or support for help regaining access.
* **Do SSO users need to set up MFA in PGP?** No. If your organization uses SSO, MFA is managed by your identity provider and you will not be prompted to set up MFA in PGP.

* For further assistance, contact your PGP administrator or support team.
